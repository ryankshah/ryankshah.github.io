Travelling By Road
From Edinburgh (35 miles) / Edinburgh Airport

Follow signs for the Forth Road Bridge/Queensferry Crossing – M90. Exit junction 2A on to A92. Follow signs to Glenrothes and the Tay Road Bridge. At Kirkcaldy end of dual carriageway go straight through 3 roundabouts still following Tay Road Bridge signs, then at fourth roundabout turn right onto the B9130 and follow signs to Markinch and Balbirnie Park.


From Perth (23 miles)

Take M90, exit Bridge of Earn at junction 9. Take A912 following signs to Glenfarg, Gateside, Strathmiglo, Falkland and Glenrothes. Just before you reach Glenrothes, turn left at roundabout signposted B9130 and follow signs for Markinch and Balbirnie Park. Alternatively take M90 to junction 3 and follow directions as from Edinburgh (this is a longer route but is motorway or dual carriageway all the way).


Travelling By Rail

The nearest train station is located in Markinch (just 5 minutes away) and it has direct links from Edinburgh's train stations. You can arrange a local taxi to get you from the station to Balbirnie House.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Can I bring a date?

Due to limited space, we can only accommodate those guests who have been formally invited on the wedding invitation. If you received a plus one, they will appear under your name when you RSVP.

Can we bring our children?

Invitations will be specific and will include the names of any children who have been invited. However, we encourage you to use this evening as a "date night" for yourself!

Can I stay at the venue?

We will notify guests of available rooms at the venue after Save The Dates are sent. However, due to a limited capacity at the wedding venue itself, please refer to our Accomodation tab for other options.

Is there a dress code?

We'd love to see our family and friends dress up with us! We are requesting your best formal attire for the evening. No jeans, t-shirts or sneakers, please! Women, please refrain from wearing white or shades of white as to not coincide with the bride.

What happens if I don't RSVP in time?

If we do not get an RSVP back by the date which we have provided, it will be marked as "Not able to attend." We will surely miss you at our wedding and celebrating this happy occasion with us, however, we have to provide total guest counts to the venue in a timely manner and cannot accept late RSVPs due to this. We hope you understand.

Can we take photos during the ceremony?

As we will have photographers at the wedding, we have a strict rule of no photographs or videos to be taken during the wedding ceremony. To put it simply, we don't want to see any phones out in our lovely photographs! However, during the evening celebrations, feel free to take as many pictures as you like and share them with us using the hashtag. #ShahsSince2024

Can we bring gifts for the bride and groom?

Having your company at our wedding is a gift in itself, however if you wish to give a gift, please consider the following poem:
"As we’ve lived together for a year or two, We really don’t need anything new. But if you were thinking of getting us a small wedding gift, Some money for our future wouldn’t go amiss."
Gifts will go towards our first home and our honeymoon.

Is there anywhere for people to smoke?

There will be specific smoking zones that are designated for any smokers that attend our wedding. These will be pointed out to those who wish to do so while at the venue. Anyone who is smoking cigarettes or e-cigarettes should be at least 5m away from any doors or windows to conform to Scottish law.
